# ADR-0001 — AGPLv3

> Francais en premier. English version below.

---

# Francais

Status

Accepted

Context

CollectionMgnt est un projet open source auto-hebergeable. Il vise a permettre a
des utilisateurs de gerer leurs collections personnelles sur leur propre
infrastructure, sans dependance a une plateforme tierce.

Le projet peut interesser des usages personnels, associatifs ou heberges. La
licence doit proteger les contributions et eviter qu'une version modifiee soit
exploitee comme service ferme sans retour vers la communaute.

Decision

CollectionMgnt utilise la licence AGPLv3.

Consequences

Positive

- Les contributions restent protegees dans les usages distribues ou heberges.
- Le choix est coherent avec un logiciel auto-hebergeable.
- Les ameliorations d'une version exposee comme service doivent rester
  accessibles selon les termes de l'AGPLv3.

Negative

- La licence peut etre moins attractive pour certains usages commerciaux fermes.
- Les contributeurs doivent comprendre les obligations propres a l'AGPLv3.
- L'integration dans certains environnements peut demander une validation
  juridique supplementaire.

# English

Status

Accepted

Context

CollectionMgnt is a self-hostable open source project. It aims to let users
manage personal collections on their own infrastructure, without depending on a
third-party platform.

The project may be used personally, by communities, or as a hosted service. The
license must protect contributions and prevent modified hosted versions from
being closed without giving improvements back to the community.

Decision

CollectionMgnt uses the AGPLv3 license.

Consequences

Positive

- Contributions remain protected in distributed and hosted use cases.
- The choice is consistent with self-hostable software.
- Improvements to a version exposed as a service must remain available under the
  AGPLv3 terms.

Negative

- The license may be less attractive for some closed commercial uses.
- Contributors must understand the obligations specific to AGPLv3.
- Integration in some environments may require additional legal review.
